Seedream 5 vs Wan 2.7 vs Nano Banana 2: price, speed, quality and content filters

Seedream 5, Wan 2.7 Image and Nano Banana 2 are three of the most capable image models available in 2026, but they solve different problems. This guide compares the versions available in VeilPix for generation, editing and multi-image composition.

The short answer

  • Choose Nano Banana 2 for the strongest all-around prompt understanding, accurate text, rapid iteration and reference consistency.
  • Choose Seedream 5 for design-heavy compositions, rich stylization, multilingual layouts and precise image editing. Lite reaches 4K; Pro emphasizes reasoning and control.
  • Choose Wan 2.7 for inexpensive high-volume generation, multi-image work and a fast standard tier. Pro adds 4K text-to-image output.
  • Choose VeilPix After Dark when an age-verified workflow needs supported safety checkers relaxed. Seedream 5 and Wan 2.7 can be more permissive with mild adult content than Nano Banana 2, but model and provider policies still apply.

Image quality and prompt following

Nano Banana 2: the best all-around default

Google positions Gemini 3.1 Flash Image, better known as Nano Banana 2, as its balance of intelligence, cost and latency. It supports 1K through 4K output, web and image-search grounding, advanced text rendering and multi-reference composition. It is the safest starting point when a job combines people, products, text and detailed instructions.

Its strongest advantage is predictable instruction following. For product mockups, posters, menus, diagrams and edits that must preserve recognizable subjects, Nano Banana 2 usually needs fewer corrective passes.

Seedream 5: deeper visual reasoning and design control

ByteDance describes Seedream 5 Lite's core improvement as deeper understanding and reasoning rather than a simple resolution or speed increase. That distinction matters. Seedream is especially compelling for editorial layouts, stylized campaigns, multilingual typography, visual explanations and edits where the model must infer design intent.

In VeilPix, Lite exposes 2K and 4K output, while Pro exposes 1K and 2K. Both support eight practical aspect ratios and PNG or JPEG output. Pro is the better choice for precision edits and complex scenes; Lite is the more economical route to high-resolution generation.

Wan 2.7: flexible production at volume

Wan 2.7 supports text-to-image, image sets, editing and multi-image references. Alibaba's standard model is the faster, lower-resolution option, while Pro adds 4K text-to-image output and stronger production positioning. Its image-set and reference workflows make it useful for campaigns that need multiple related assets rather than one hero image.

Wan can be less consistent than Nano Banana 2 on intricate typography or highly constrained edits, but its throughput, multi-image features and low official base price make it a strong workhorse.

Content filtering and mild NSFW workflows

No reputable model is literally uncensored. All three remain subject to law, platform rules and non-adjustable protections for core harms. The practical difference is how aggressively borderline adult material is blocked.

  • Nano Banana 2 is the strictest of the three. Google exposes adjustable safety categories in the Gemini API, but the model also has built-in protections and Google's prohibited-use policy disallows sexually explicit content created for sexual gratification.
  • Seedream 5 and Wan 2.7 can be more permissive for mild adult content when an API integration supports disabling its optional NSFW checker. Results are still not guaranteed, and explicit or prohibited material may be refused upstream.
  • VeilPix After Dark is limited to age-verified accounts. Purchasing credits provides the verification step; users can then enable After Dark for direct access to supported model APIs with optional safety checkers relaxed. This does not bypass model rules or legal restrictions.

For boudoir, swimwear, figure studies or other lawful mature creative work, Seedream 5 or Wan 2.7 is usually the more practical first attempt. For mainstream commercial work involving sensitive imagery, Nano Banana 2's stricter behavior may be preferable.
